# Cold Email Structure

## Subject line
- 3-6 words. Specific to the business or the pain point, not the offer name.
- Never "Quick question" or "Following up" — overused, filtered by spam tools and readers alike.
- Good pattern: "[specific observation about their business]" or "missed messages at [Business]"

## Body structure (in order)

1. **Opening line — specific, not about me.** Reference something real about their business (from their site, their sector, their hours). Proves this isn't a mail merge.
2. **The problem, stated plainly.** One sentence connecting that observation to a cost they already feel (lost bookings, slow replies, after-hours messages going unanswered).
3. **What I built, one sentence.** Name the thing, not a feature list. "I build WhatsApp/Telegram systems that answer, book, and follow up with customers automatically — the way a receptionist would."
4. **Proof link.** One line, one link to the demo. No description needed beyond "here's a 60-second example."
5. **The ask.** Single sentence, low friction. "Worth a quick look?" or "Want me to show you what this would look like for [Business] specifically?"
6. **Sign-off.** Name, one-line identity (see below), unsubscribe line.

## Required identity line (PECR compliance)

Every email includes, near the sign-off, plain and unhidden:
"I'm Mike, a Nigeria-based automation specialist — I build this myself, not part of an agency."

## Required opt-out line (PECR compliance)

Every email ends with a working, honest opt-out — not buried, not tiny grey text designed to be missed:
"Not relevant to you? Reply 'no thanks' and I won't email again."

This must actually be honored — any "no thanks" or unsubscribe reply removes that business from all future sends permanently.

## What NOT to do

- No attachments in a first-touch email — spam filters penalize this heavily and most people won't open them from a stranger.
- No more than one link.
- No images embedded in the email body — they hurt deliverability on first contact and often get stripped anyway.
- No fake urgency ("limited spots," "only 3 left this week") — untrue and it reads as untrue.
