# Hooks & Angles: AI Automation

Pick one angle per email based on what's actually observable about that business — don't force a hook that isn't backed by something real on their site.

## Angle 1: After-hours gap
For businesses with clear opening hours listed but no after-hours contact method.
Hook: messages sent outside opening hours currently just wait until the business reopens — meaning anyone in a hurry has already gone elsewhere.

## Angle 2: Single point of contact overload
For salons/clinics where one person is clearly doing both service and reception (small team, single number).
Hook: every message answered mid-appointment is either a distracted customer in the chair or a missed message — neither is good.

## Angle 3: Competitor response speed
For trades — response speed genuinely determines who gets the job.
Hook: the business that replies to a callout request in under a minute usually gets it, regardless of price. Most trades can't hit that consistently by hand.

## Structure for using any angle

State the observation → name the cost in one sentence → introduce the fix in one sentence → link the demo. Don't stack multiple angles in one email — pick the one that fits this specific business best from what's visible on their site.
