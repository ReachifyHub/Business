# Objections: AI Automation

These are for reply handling, not the cold email itself — but useful context for the AI to have when drafting follow-ups.

## "We already reply fast enough"
Don't argue. Ask what their current after-hours/weekend coverage looks like specifically — that's usually the actual gap, even when daytime response is fine.

## "Sounds expensive" / "What's the real cost"
Reframe against what one missed booking is worth to them, not against the fee in isolation. Don't discount immediately — first response should clarify value, not drop price.

## "How is this different from [other chatbot tool]"
Most generic chatbot tools handle fixed FAQ trees. This one is built around their actual business and can hold a real conversation, take a booking, and hand off to the owner — not a decision-tree bot. Point to the demo again rather than a feature war.

## "Are you a real company / can I trust this"
Be straightforwardly honest: solo-built, Nigeria-based, direct point of contact (no support ticket queue), happy to demo live before any payment changes hands. Don't oversell size or team that doesn't exist.

## "I don't have time to set this up"
That's the point of the offer — one week, they provide basic business info (hours, services, FAQs), the building is on me.
