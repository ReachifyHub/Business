# Audience: AI Automation

## Starting sectors (both, running in parallel — not sequential)

### Trades
Plumbers, electricians, locksmiths, and similar. A missed message or unanswered call means the customer moves to the next name on Google immediately — there's no loyalty holding them. Urgency is already built into how these customers behave, which makes "you're losing jobs to slow replies" a true, provable claim, not a stretch.

### Salons, barbers, clinics
Booking-driven businesses with no dedicated reception staff. The owner or a stylist/practitioner is doing double duty answering messages between clients, which means slow replies and missed messages during business hours, not just after hours.

## Why these two first

Both have an obvious, immediate cost to slow replies that the owner already feels — no need to convince them a problem exists, only that this solves it. Sharper targeting means sharper, more specific personalization per email, which is the single biggest lever on reply rate at this stage (see core-principles.md).

## Not yet (round two candidates)

Restaurants, retail — broader audience, less universally urgent pain point. Revisit once there's real proof (a paying client or strong demo response) from the first two sectors.

## What to look for per business before emailing

- Do they list a phone number or WhatsApp as primary contact, with no chatbot already in use
- Evening/weekend hours (signals after-hours messages are likely) 
- Any visible sign of being understaffed for reception (single-location, small team implied by site)

If a business already has an obvious existing chatbot or booking system, skip — not worth the reply-rate cost of pitching something they'd view as redundant.
